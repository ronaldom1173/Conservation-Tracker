Animal Conservation Status Tracker 
Use the IUCN Red List API and GBIF (Global Biodiversity Information Facility) data. Track which species are improving vs. declining, what regions are biodiversity 
hotspots vs. losing the most species, and whether conservation spending correlates with outcomes. This one tells a meaningful story.

Data sources (all free)

    IUCN Red List API — conservation status (Least Concern → Extinct), population trends, threats, habitat info. Requires a free API token (takes a day or two to get approved).
    GBIF (Global Biodiversity Information Facility) — species occurrence records, geographic distribution. Massive dataset, no auth needed for basic use.
    Our World in Data — has clean CSVs on biodiversity, protected area coverage, conservation spending by country.
    World Bank Open Data — country-level GDP, land use, protected area %, for cross-referencing

questions to answer

Which taxonomic groups (mammals, birds, amphibians, reptiles, fish, insects) are most threatened, and how has that shifted over the last 20 years?
Where are the biodiversity hotspots vs. the "extinction hotspots"?
Does a country's protected-area coverage or conservation spending actually correlate with better species outcomes?
Which species have improved status (success stories like the bald eagle, humpback whale) — what do they have in common?
Are there species that moved from "Least Concern" straight to "Endangered" recently — i.e., rapid decliners that deserve attention?
